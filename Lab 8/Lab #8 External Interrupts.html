Description

This lab demonstrates the use of interrupts on the ATmega 2560. An interrupt, also known as an Interrupt Service Routine (ISR), is a special function that is called by the microcontroller when certain conditions are present (such as when an assigned pushbutton has been pressed). These functions can be used to interface with hardware, or to ensure a function is called at regular intervals using the timer hardware.

Your Assignment

  1. Set up the registers for the interrupt
  2. Create an interrupt subroutine to index an optical encoder

Hardware

You will be using the GM9236S019 DC Servo Gearmotor along with a power supply for this lab. You must exercise caution when running the motor so as not to burn it out. The voltage is not to exceed 5V and the current is not to exceed 50mA. To begin, turn the current knob all the way to the left. This will cause a short and drop your voltage near zero. Slowly turn the current knob to the right until you have a reading of not more than 50mA. Then adjust your voltage to 5 volts.
